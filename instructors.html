Our Instructors

Chris Como (Club Director)

Chris was a former Burrell Wrestling Alum, where he was a 3-year starter, compiling a 70-19-1 High School record. He won two Section Titles and was a two-time Southwest Regional Placewinner, finishing 4th his Junior year and was the AAA WPIAL runner-up at 145 lbs. his Senior Year. He went onto the PIAA AAA State Tournament in 1989 where he finished in the top 8. Chris then moved on to the University of Pittsburgh-Johnstown, where he was a four-year starter for the Mountain cats, Team Captain in 1994, and compiled a record of 81-36-2. Chris went on to become a 3x NCAA Northeast Regional Placewinner, finishing 3rd in 1992 and 1993 while finishing runner-up in 1994. Qualifying for the 1994 Div II NCAA Championships, Chris went on to place 8th and become an All-American for the 1st time. After his competitive career, Chris went directly into coaching. In 1996, he assisted Pat Pecora to winning UPJ's first-ever National Title. In 1996-1997, he joined the Burrell Coaching staff as an Assistant where the Bucs won their first WPIAL Team Championship. Chris took over as Head Coach in the 1998 season and enjoyed over a decade of success at Burrell, where he compiled a record of 141-43. During that time, Chris Coached 4 WPIAL Championship teams (2004, 2007, 2008, 2009) and the WPIAL's only AA Team State Title in 2008. Chris was named WPIAL AA Coach of the Year four times (2004, 2005, 2007, 2008) and PIAA State AA Coach of the Year in 2008. Chris's Burrell Teams also captured Southwest AA Regional Team Titles in 2001, 2005 and 2007. During Chris's tenure as Burrell's Head Coach, he has also coached 23 WPIAL Individual Champions, 26 State Placewinners, 10 State Finalists and 4 State Champions. In his professional life, Chris has been a Software Engineer/Administrator for Highmark, Inc. for the past 19 years. Chris and his wife, Angela, have three boys, Christian, Luke, and Anthony.

Isaac Greeley (Club Director)

Isaac was a standout wrestler for Port Allegany High School, where he was a 3x District 9 Champion and voted Outstanding Wrestler at the District 9 Tournament. Isaac was also a 4x PIAA AA State Qualifier and 2x PIAA AA State Placewinner, finishing 3rd and 5th in Pennsylvania. Isaac continued his wrestling career, collegiately, at Pitt-Johnstown University, where he collected 3 NCAA East Regional Titles and was a 2x NCAA All-American, finishing as Runner-up in 1998 and 3rd in 1999. Isaac also excelled in the classroom and was named to the NCAA All-Academic First Team twice. Isaac was also a member of both UPJ Wrestling teams to bring home National Championships in 1996 and 1999. Isaac went on to coach after his competitive years, as a member of the UPJ Coaching staff in 2000 and 2001, where he coached NCAA Champions, Brandon Newill and Shad Benton. He moved to the A-K Valley and shortly thereafter joined Chris Como's coaching staff at Burrell High School in 2003-04. Isaac was a key component in the long string of success that the Bucs enjoyed, up until this day. The Bucs have been to the WPIAL Championship every year since Isaac joined the staff and have brought home 7 WPIAL Titles with Isaac's guidance. Isaac was also part of the staff that won the 2008 PIAA Team Title as well as the 2011 Team that won the Team Title at the PIAA Individual State Championships. Isaac has also had a hand in 22 WPIAL Champions, 26 State Placewinners, 7 State Finalists and 3 State Champions. Isaac has also been inducted into the Pennsylvania Youth Wrestling Hall of Fame. Professionally, Isaac is a Doctor of Chiropractic Medicine and runs the Rehab Centres out of Lower Burrell, Kittanning, Apollo and Clarion. Isaac and his wife, Laura, have a young daughter, Seneca.

Jordan Shields (Club Coach and Youth Development Instructor)

The Mat Factory Jordan Shields Jordan joined the Mat Factory Coaching staff after a decorative Collegiate and High School Wrestling career. In his years as a Burrell High School grappler, Jordan attained a career record of 165-27. He was one of a handful of 4x WPIAL Champions. He also was a 3x PIAA Southwest Regional Champion. Jordan won a PIAA State Title in 2007 as a Junior and finished as the PIAA State Runner-up in 2008. He also captured the 140 lb. title at the Ironman Tournament, largely considered the toughest High School Wrestling tournament in the Country. Jordan also served as Team Captain of the 2008 Burrell High School team, the PIAA AA State Team Champions that season. After his highly-touted High School campaign, Jordan followed his brother Josh's footsteps to Mercyhurst University, where he went on to become the Super Region 1 Champion and in that same year became an All-American for the first time, placing 4th at the NCAA D. II National Championships. Jordan excelled in the classroom at Mercyhurst as well, graduating with a degree in Sports Medicine and specializing in Exercise Science with a 3.1 GPA... Good enough to earn him 3x Academic All-American honors, once a 1st Team Academic All-American. Jordan currently works for the Rehab Centre as Rehab Director and also performs personal training and Private Wrestling sessions. Jordan recently married his wife Ashley.
